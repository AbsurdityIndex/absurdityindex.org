## Key Learnings

### Twitter/X Constraints
- **Polls cannot have images** — Must use thread workaround (image tweet → poll reply)
- **X Articles don't support embedded polls** — Premium+ Articles aren't suitable for interactive content
- **25 character limit per poll option** — Keep options punchy

### Content Strategy
- **Non-partisan is crucial** — Political content that leans partisan will alienate half the audience
- **AI angle is differentiating** — Most political satire accounts don't emphasize their tech stack
- **Procedural humor works** — Senate "legislative days" and bill naming conventions are absurd enough to be funny without being divisive

### Repost Criteria
- Must understand full context before reposting
- Avoid anything that could be seen as partisan
- AI/tech content fits because the site is AI-powered
- Community testimonials build social proof

## Monetization Strategy — The Road to $5 (and Beyond)

### X Ad Revenue Sharing — How It Works
- X shares ad revenue from **ads displayed in reply threads** to your posts
- Requirements: **X Premium or Premium+** (already have Premium+), **500+ followers**, **5M+ organic impressions in last 3 months**
- Payouts are monthly via Stripe once you hit the minimum threshold

### Where We Stand
- **Premium+:** Already subscribed
- **Followers:** Need to check current count — 500 is the floor
- **Impressions:** This is the grind — need to hit 5M in 3 months

### What Actually Generates Revenue
Revenue comes from ads in **reply threads** on your original posts. This means:
1. **Original posts that spark replies** are the money-makers, not reposts
2. **Reposts build audience** but don't directly earn (no reply thread = no ad placement)
3. **Controversial-but-not-toxic takes** generate the most replies
4. **Polls and questions** are reply magnets

### Content Playbook for Monetization

**High-reply-potential post formats:**
- "Rate this bill's absurdity 1-10" — forces people to reply with a number
- "What would YOU name this bill?" — creative engagement
- "This bill passed. Guess how many pages it was." — curiosity gap
- Hot takes on bill names vs. what they actually do — people love correcting or agreeing
- "Congress did X today. Is this more or less absurd than Y?" — comparison debates

**The repost strategy still matters:**
- Reposts grow follower count → more people see original posts → more replies → more ad revenue
- Think of reposts as **audience building** and original posts as **revenue generating**

### Realistic Revenue Timeline

| Milestone | What It Takes | Estimated Revenue |
|-----------|---------------|-------------------|
| Month 1-2 | Build to 500 followers, post daily | $0 (not eligible yet) |
| Month 3-4 | Hit 500 followers, grind impressions | $0-5 (just becoming eligible) |
| Month 5-6 | Consistent posting, 1-2 viral hits | $5-50/month |
| Month 6-12 | Established voice, regular engagement | $50-200/month |
| Year 2+ | Known account in niche, automation pipeline | $200-500+/month |

**The honest truth:** $5 is very achievable within a few months. Getting rich requires either going massively viral or playing the long game with consistent, engagement-optimized content.

### Quick Wins to Start Earning Faster
1. **Post during peak hours** — 8-10am and 7-9pm ET (when DC/political Twitter is active)
2. **Quote-tweet instead of plain repost** — adds your commentary, creates a reply thread on YOUR post
3. **Reply to trending political topics** with the absurdity angle — ride the wave
4. **Thread your best content** — first tweet hooks, rest of thread keeps people engaged (ads show in long threads)
5. **Pin your best-performing tweet** — keeps generating impressions passively

### What NOT to Do (Engagement Farming Traps)
- Don't steal content/memes — gets you reported and deplatformed
- Don't post rage bait — burns audience trust and doesn't fit the brand
- Don't buy followers — fake followers don't reply, kills your engagement rate
- Don't post 20x a day — quality over quantity, algorithm penalizes low-engagement posts

### Automation Revenue Ideas (Future)
- Auto-generate daily "absurdity score" posts from new bills on the site
- Weekly automated thread: "This week in congressional absurdity"
- Bot that monitors congress.gov for new bills and drafts satirical tweets for review
- These create **consistent content** without daily manual effort = sustainable impressions
